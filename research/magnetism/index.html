The usual way physicists classify magnetic materials is by using two main classes: ferromagnets and antiferromagnets. Ferromagnets display a large net magnetic moment and a hysteretic behaviour as a function of the applied magnetic field. On the other hand, antiferromagnetism has no macroscopic magnetic moment, despite being microscopically ordered. It was indeed surprising that a complete classification of the spin-group symmetries included not two, but three categories: ferromagnetism, antiferromagnetism and altermagnetism This novel notion was, so to speak, hiding in plain sight. https://doi.org/10.1002/adma.202211966
